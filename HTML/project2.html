DR. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Born on 15th October 1931 at Rameswaram in Tamil Nadu, Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, specialized in Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology. Dr. Kalam made significant contribution as Project Director to develop India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully injected the Rohini satellite in the near earth orbit in July 1980 and made India an exclusive member of Space Club. He was responsible for the evolution of ISRO's launch vehicle programme, particularly the PSLV configuration.

timeline of Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam’s life

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam was deeply inspired by his parents, teachers, great scientists, and Indian leaders who shaped his values of simplicity, discipline, and service to the nation. These inspirations guided him to dream big, work hard, and dedicate his life to India’s development.

  • 1931-Inspired by his father Jainulabdeen’s honesty and simplicity
  • 1938-Inspired by his mother Ashiamma’s kindness and moral values
  • 1945-Inspired by his school teacher Sivasubramania Iyer
  • 1950-Inspired by books on science and aviation
  • 1954-Inspired by Indian freedom movement leaders
  • 1958-Inspired by aircraft and flight during MIT studies
  • 1962-Inspired by Dr. Vikram Sarabhai at ISRO
  • 1965-Inspired by Dr. Satish Dhawan’s leadership and humility
  • 1970 – Inspired by teamwork and failure as a learning process
  • 1980 – Inspired by India’s first successful satellite launch
  • 1990 – Inspired by youth and students across India
  • 2002 – Inspired by the responsibility of serving as President
  • 2010 – Inspired by teaching and interacting with young minds
